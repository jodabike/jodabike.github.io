*July 10 - July 12*

We left our hostel on Wednesday morning after the hostel owner gave us a bunch of herbs of his garden which help against stomach pain and having a long chat with his two really nice and interested kids.

<!--more-->

As Jo was not 100% fit yet, we only rode a short asphalt distance that day.

<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180659-1.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-821" width="4608" height="3456">

We had dinner at a cozy restaurant with good mountain view and could set up our tent next to it.

<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180683.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-751" width="4261" height="3200"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_4842.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-752" width="4032" height="3024">

The next morning, we turned to a dirt road to go over some beautiful passes again. It was a lonely road, except for about 20 tourist busses that passed us in the morning to bring tourists from Huaraz to the Pastoruri glacier. A glacier that has shrunk to less than one third of the size it had in 1980 and will probably not be there anymore in 20 years.

<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_3834.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-750" width="3284" height="2367">

<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_3838.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-753" width="4032" height="3024">

We took a break in the middle of nowhere, when suddenly the sweetest little lamb appeared out of the grassland, came running towards us and jumped right into our arms.

<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_3843.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-754" width="4032" height="3024"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_3849.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-757" width="4032" height="3024"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_4856.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-756" width="4032" height="3024">

It seemed really desperate and hungry and even looked for tits on our bikes. <img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180693.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-755" width="4608" height="3456">

As there were no other sheep anywhere around us, we decided to take the little guy with us for about 5 km and bring it to the entrance of the national park. <img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_3855.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-758" width="3024" height="4032"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_3858.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-759" width="3024" height="4032">

We first tried it Peruvian style and wrapped it in a sheet, but as we are Germans, we just get along better with backpacks. ;)<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180697.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-760" width="4608" height="3456"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180709.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-761" width="4608" height="3456"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180711.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-762" width="4608" height="3456">

At the entrance of the national park, the guards were happy that we brought the lamb with us and prepared some milk for it right away. They knew the farmer and would bring the little guy back to its mum soon.

We rode on through the national park and passed the funniest looking plants.

<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180745.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-765" width="4608" height="3456"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180752.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-763" width="3456" height="4608"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180724.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-764" width="4608" height="3456">

On our way, we met two friendly German cyclists, on their way through South America as well, and had a long chat with them (They also have a blog: rumradeln.de). Then we rode on through the beautiful park and set up camp in the evening.

<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180759.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-769" width="4608" height="3456"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180762.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-768" width="4608" height="3456"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180774.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-767" width="4608" height="3456">After a cold night up on 4300 m, we headed further uphill the next morning.<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180781.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-773" width="4608" height="3456"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180794.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-772" width="4608" height="3456"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180812.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-771" width="4608" height="3456"><img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/img_3882-1.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-770" width="4032" height="3024">

We passed the Pasturori glacier, but only looked at it from the far distance as there is not much to see anymore and we had plenty of other beautiful glaciers around us.

<img src="https://ohwieschoenistpatagonia.files.wordpress.com/2019/07/p1180815.jpg" class="size-full wp-image-774" width="4608" height="3456">

Shortly after, we reached an amazing high plateau at about 4700 m on which we cycled along for the rest of the day.

And we know, we say this a lot, but as so often up there in the Andes, the view was amazing! :)
